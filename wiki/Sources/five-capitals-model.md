## Summary

The Five Capitals Model, developed by Forum for the Future, provides a basis for understanding sustainability in terms of the economic concept of wealth creation or "capital." Any organisation uses five types of capital to deliver its products or services. A sustainable organisation will maintain and where possible enhance these stocks of capital assets, rather than deplete or degrade them.

The five capitals are:

1. **Natural capital** — the natural resources and ecological systems that provide goods and services essential to life. "Natural capital is the basis not only of production but of life itself!"
2. **Human capital** — the health, knowledge, skills, and motivation of individuals. "Enhancing human capital through education and training is central to a flourishing economy."
3. **Social capital** — the networks, relationships, trust, and institutions that enable cooperation
4. **Manufactured capital** — the material goods, infrastructure, and tools that contribute to production
5. **Financial capital** — the monetary resources that enable the other four capitals to be owned and traded

The core insight is that we are facing a sustainability crisis because "we're consuming our stocks of natural, human and social capital faster than they are being produced." Unless we control the rate of consumption, we cannot sustain these vital stocks long-term. By maintaining and increasing stocks of all five capitals, we can live off the income without reducing the capital itself.

The model includes 12 features describing what a sustainable society should look like, fitted across the five capitals. These help organisations evaluate the sustainability of their projects and strategies.

## Key Claims

- Sustainability means maintaining and enhancing all five capital stocks, not converting natural/social capital into financial capital
- Natural capital is the basis of life itself, not merely a production input
- The sustainability crisis stems from consuming natural, human, and social capital faster than they are produced
- Living off income rather than depleting capital is the core principle
