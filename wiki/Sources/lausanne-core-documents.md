## Summary

The Lausanne core documents are four foundational statements that have guided evangelical Christian mission globally since 1974. Each emerged from a major international congress of church leaders and represents the evolving understanding of holistic mission within global evangelicalism.

**The Lausanne Covenant (1974)** — The founding document, drafted at the first International Congress on World Evangelization convened by Billy Graham in Lausanne, Switzerland. It affirmed the inseparability of evangelism and social responsibility, marking a watershed moment for evangelical mission thinking. Its influence on global evangelicalism has been compared to Vatican II for Catholicism.

**The Manila Manifesto (1989)** — Built on the Covenant, emphasising holistic mission, societal transformation, and the proclamation of the gospel. It addressed emerging issues of the late 20th century including urbanisation, poverty, and religious pluralism.

**The Cape Town Commitment (2010)** — The most comprehensive of the four, structured as a confession of faith and a call to action. It explicitly integrates creation care, justice, and reconciliation into the mission agenda and includes the foundational statement: "we cannot separate our relationship to Christ from how we act in relation to the earth."

**The Seoul Statement (2024)** — The most recent, addressing mission in the context of the 2020s: digital transformation, AI, climate crisis, pandemic aftermath, and shifting global Christianity toward the Global South.

Together, these documents represent a 50-year legacy shaping how evangelical Christians worldwide approach missionary work, addressing "core beliefs and strategies for global missions, emphasizing evangelism, discipleship, and social engagement."

## Key Claims

- The Lausanne Covenant (1974) affirmed the inseparability of evangelism and social responsibility
- Each successive document expanded the scope of mission: from proclamation to justice, creation care, and reconciliation
- The Cape Town Commitment (2010) explicitly integrated creation care into mission
- The movement represents 50 years of evolving evangelical mission theology
