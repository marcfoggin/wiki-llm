## Summary

Cris Rogers sets out five reasons why Christians should love their neighbours and serve, grounding each in Scripture and the example of Christ.

**1. Jesus commanded it.** Both through his words and his example, Jesus made love and service central to the life of faith. The command is not optional but foundational.

**2. We love what God loves.** God values all people, and believers are called to see divine worth in every human being. Loving others reflects God's own character.

**3. Service incarnates Christ's love.** Acts of service make Christ's love tangible and visible in the world. They are not merely good deeds but expressions of the gospel.

**4. Serving others is serving Jesus.** Drawing on Matthew 25:40 — "When you do it for the least of these you are doing it for me" — Rogers argues that every act of service is directed toward Christ himself.

**5. Authentic love creates credibility.** Demonstrated sincerity through loving action creates opportunities to share the gospel that words alone cannot. People respond to love they can see and experience.

The piece is pastoral rather than academic, aimed at mobilising church communities toward practical service. It frames service not as a strategy for evangelism but as an expression of genuine love that naturally opens doors.

## Key Claims

- Service is commanded by Jesus, not optional for Christians
- Every act of service is directed toward Christ himself (Matthew 25:40)
- Incarnational love — made visible through action — creates credibility for witness
- Service is an expression of love, not a strategy for ulterior purposes
