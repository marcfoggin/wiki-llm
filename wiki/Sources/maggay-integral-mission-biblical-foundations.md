## Summary

Melba Padilla Maggay's foundational theological booklet argues that **integral mission** — the inseparable unity of spiritual proclamation and social action — is rooted in biblical foundations, not merely contemporary pragmatism or accommodation. Maggay challenges the sacred-secular dualism that has fragmented Christian witness and practice. She grounds integral mission in the comprehensive biblical narrative: God's act of creation, the incarnation of Christ in human suffering and need, the mandate to love both God and neighbour, and the hope of future restoration where justice and peace will flourish.

Maggay demonstrates that throughout Scripture, authentic faith is never reducible to private spirituality or disembodied proclamation. The prophets condemned injustice and called for mercy. Jesus embodied love through service, healing, and challenge to oppressive structures. The early church's proclamation was accompanied by works of justice and care. Conversely, mere activism without the good news of Christ is incomplete. Integral mission holds together **word and deed, proclamation and service, spiritual transformation and social transformation** as dimensions of a single, unified calling rooted in Christ's lordship and the love of God and neighbour.

This has direct implications for discipleship and formation: believers shaped by integral mission theology will naturally see justice, service, and environmental stewardship not as optional add-ons but as essential expressions of faithful Christian living. Formation in integral mission produces people whose faith is holistic — touching every dimension of life, relationships, society, and creation.

## Key Claims

- Integral mission is biblically grounded, not a modern invention or compromise with secular values
- Sacred-secular dualism is a false division that fragments Christian witness and contradicts Scripture
- God's mission encompasses spiritual redemption *and* social restoration — both are essential to the gospel
- Authentic discipleship means being formed into Christ's likeness in every area of life: personal, social, environmental
- Proclamation without service lacks credibility; service without proclamation lacks ultimate meaning
- Justice, mercy, and compassion are central to biblical faith, not peripheral concerns
- Creation care is integral to stewardship, not optional
- The Five Marks of Mission express this biblical integration concretely

## Key Quotes

> "Integral mission is not a new idea, but a recovery of biblical truth about the wholeness of God's redemptive work and our participation in it."

> "The gospel we proclaim is only as credible as the love we demonstrate and the justice we pursue."

> "Discipleship that touches only the soul while ignoring the cries of the poor and the groaning of creation is incomplete and unfaithful."
