## Summary

The Pastoralist Knowledge Hub is a technical resource and impartial discussion space managed by the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO). It functions as a centralised platform designed to bridge significant gaps in international policy conversations regarding pastoralism. By consolidating diverse knowledge materials — scholarly literature, technical instruments, evaluations, and empirical investigations — the Hub facilitates evidence-based policy development and advocates for approaches that support pastoral communities.

The Hub provides comprehensive resources spanning multiple formats: publications, instructional videos, and legislative documents relevant to pastoral systems. It hosts the Pastoralist Forum, maintains regional network connections, and manages a database of partner organisations. The news section features "Pastoralist Conversations" amplifying individual voices and experiences from pastoral communities worldwide — shepherds from Spain, buffalo herders from Thailand, pastoral women from Tunisia and Mongolia.

Global pastoral populations are substantial: approximately two billion individuals — encompassing pastoralists, small-scale herders, ranchers, and farmers — depend on healthy rangelands internationally. These communities are particularly vital in challenging agricultural regions including drylands, mountains, and shrublands where conventional crop production faces significant obstacles.

The Hub addresses interconnected policy dimensions including rangeland management, water tenure, land governance, biodiversity conservation, women's empowerment in pastoral households, and climate adaptation. A major initiative is the 2026 International Year of Rangelands and Pastoralists, which aims to "raise awareness of the importance of rangelands and pastoralists, and to advocate for increased" investment and policy recognition.

## Key Claims

- Approximately two billion people depend on healthy rangelands globally
- Pastoralists are vital in drylands, mountains, and shrublands where conventional agriculture struggles
- Policy gaps in international discussions systematically disadvantage pastoral communities
- 2026 is the International Year of Rangelands and Pastoralists
