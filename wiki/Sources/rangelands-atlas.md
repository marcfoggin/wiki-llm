## Summary

The Rangelands Atlas is a collaborative resource documenting rangeland ecosystems globally through interactive maps and detailed case studies. Created through partnerships among the FAO, UNEP, IUCN, and WWF, it provides spatial data on the extent, condition, and importance of the world's rangelands.

These landscapes "are home to millions of people" and "feed millions of people worldwide" while supporting diverse wildlife and providing cultural value. The atlas highlights significant changes occurring in rangelands — including degradation, conversion, and climate impacts — that necessitate their protection and restoration.

The atlas serves as an evidence base for policy advocacy, making visible the scale and significance of ecosystems that are routinely overlooked in conservation and climate policy. It connects spatial data with narratives about pastoral livelihoods, biodiversity, and carbon storage.

## Key Claims

- Rangelands are home to and feed millions of people while supporting biodiversity and cultural value
- Significant changes in rangeland extent and condition require urgent protection and restoration
- Spatial data makes rangeland importance visible for policy-makers who otherwise overlook them
