## Summary

The International Year of Rangelands and Pastoralists (IYRP) 2026 is a global initiative aimed at raising awareness about the critical importance of rangelands and the people who steward them. Backed by 102 countries and 430 organisations, it is coordinated by the FAO alongside a diverse coalition including the Mongolian National Support Group, NGOs, and regional pastoral communities.

The vision is that "the economic, environmental and social importance of rangelands, and of pastoralists as their custodians, are fully recognized, rewarded, safeguarded and protected worldwide." The initiative recognises how rangeland ecosystems support livelihoods for hundreds of millions while preserving biodiversity and combating climate change.

IYRP 2026 provides a unique policy window for advancing pastoral rights, rangeland governance, and investment in the ecosystems that cover over half the world's land surface. It connects local pastoral communities with global policy frameworks and advocacy platforms.
