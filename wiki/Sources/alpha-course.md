## Summary

Alpha is a widely used introductory course exploring the Christian faith, designed for people who are curious but not sure about faith. It runs in churches, homes, cafes, and online in over 100 countries and 100+ languages. Each course runs over several weeks with a consistent three-part structure.

Each session begins with time to connect and eat together — food is usually provided free by the host church or community. A talk follows (either live or as a video episode), designed to spark conversation and unpack a different question about the Christian faith. The Alpha talks give an overview of key Christian beliefs across topics including purpose, Jesus, faith, prayer, the Bible, and the Holy Spirit.

The most important part of any Alpha is the open conversation that follows — a safe space where participants can share thoughts and questions, or simply listen. "There's no pressure to say anything or come to any conclusions — just a safe space for everyone's unique perspective."

Alpha's approach is relational and non-pressured: food creates welcome, content creates a shared reference point, and discussion creates space for honest exploration. It has become one of the most recognised discipleship entry points globally, with a youth version (Alpha Youth Series) and various filmed series for different contexts.

## Key Claims

- Designed for seekers and the curious, not the already convinced
- Three-part structure: meal, talk, open discussion
- No pressure to agree or conclude — emphasises safe space
- Runs in 100+ countries and 100+ languages
- Relational approach: welcome through food, content, and honest conversation
