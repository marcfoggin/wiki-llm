## Overview

The second mark of mission: to teach, baptise, and nurture new believers. Discipleship goes beyond initial conversion — it is the lifelong process of being formed into the likeness of Christ. Formation involves learning, community, practice, and transformation of character.

Chris Wright connects teaching directly to the Great Commission — "make disciples, baptizing them … and teaching them" — and links it to Christ's Lordship. Teaching means bringing people "into maturity of faith and discipleship, in submission to Christ as Lord." The goal is not mere intellectual assent to doctrine but lived obedience and transformation of character that reflects Christ's values.

In contexts of development and conservation, discipleship shapes how believers relate to creation, to the poor, and to unjust structures. A person's formation determines whether their faith engagement is holistic or fragmented. Formation rooted in Christ's Lordship over all creation produces believers who see justice, service, and environmental care as integral to faithfulness, not optional extras.

## Key Claims

- Discipleship is lifelong formation, not a one-time event
- Spiritual formation shapes how believers engage with justice, creation, and community
- Courses and resources exist across traditions to support structured formation journeys
