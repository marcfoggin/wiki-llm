## Overview

Traditional and indigenous livelihoods embody generations of adaptive knowledge about managing ecosystems. These ways of life are not relics but living systems that sustain both communities and biodiversity. Indigenous peoples manage a significant share of the world's land, and their territories often overlap with areas of high conservation value. Recognising and supporting these systems is critical for both justice and sustainability.

## Key Claims

- Indigenous and local communities govern territories that are among the most biodiverse on Earth
- "Territories of life" — areas governed by indigenous and local communities — are key to global environmental sustainability
- Food systems rooted in indigenous knowledge tend to be more diverse and resilient than industrial monocultures
- Dispossession of indigenous lands leads to both social harm and ecological degradation
