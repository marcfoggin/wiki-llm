## Overview

Protected areas are a primary tool for biodiversity conservation, but their effectiveness depends heavily on governance. Historically, many protected areas were established using exclusionary models that displaced indigenous and local communities. More recent approaches recognise that conservation outcomes improve when local people are included as partners and rights-holders.

This is especially relevant in pastoralist landscapes, where mobile livelihoods require access to large, connected territories that often overlap with protected area boundaries. Conflict between conservation goals and pastoral mobility is common but not inevitable — collaborative governance models can support both biodiversity and livelihoods.

## Key Claims

- Protected areas cover around 17% of the world's land [needs verification], but coverage alone does not guarantee effective conservation
- Exclusionary ("fortress") conservation models often harm local communities without improving ecological outcomes
- Indigenous and community conserved areas (ICCAs) can be as effective or more effective than state-managed protected areas
- Mobile pastoralism and protected areas can coexist through collaborative governance and corridor connectivity

## Key Frameworks & Standards

- [IUCN Protected Area Categories](https://www.iucn.org/theme/protected-areas/about/protected-area-categories) — International classification system defining six categories of protected area, ranging from strict nature reserves (Category Ia) to protected areas with sustainable use of natural resources (Category VI). The system recognises diverse governance types including community-managed areas.
- [A primer on governance for protected and conserved areas](https://portals.iucn.org/library/node/44874) — IUCN guidance on governance quality and diversity in conservation, covering authority, accountability, and rights-based approaches.
- [Recognising ICCAs overlapped by protected areas](https://portals.iucn.org/library/sites/library/files/documents/PAG-034-En.pdf) — IUCN guidance on how to recognise and respect territories conserved by indigenous peoples and local communities when these overlap with formal protected areas.
