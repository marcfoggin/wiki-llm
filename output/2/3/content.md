## Overview

Rangelands — including grasslands, savannas, shrublands, and tundra — cover roughly half the world's land surface and are critical for biodiversity, carbon storage, and food security. Yet they are among the most undervalued and under-protected ecosystems. Biodiversity loss in these areas directly undermines the livelihoods of hundreds of millions of people, particularly pastoralists and smallholders in marginal environments.

Mountain biodiversity is especially significant: mountains host disproportionate species richness and provide ecosystem services — water, pollination, climate regulation — that extend far beyond their borders.

## Key Claims

- Biodiversity loss is a development issue, not just an environmental one — it undermines food security, water supply, and resilience
- Grasslands provide ecosystem services comparable to forests but receive a fraction of conservation attention
- Pastoralism can actively mitigate biodiversity loss on rangelands through sustainable grazing and mobility
- Mountain biodiversity is central to sustainable development both in mountains and downstream
