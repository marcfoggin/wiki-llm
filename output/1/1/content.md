## Overview

The first mark of mission: to proclaim the good news of the Kingdom. Evangelism is the announcement that God's reign has broken into the world through Jesus Christ, bringing reconciliation, hope, and transformation. It is not reducible to a technique or programme but is the church's witness to what God has done and is doing.

Within the Five Marks framework, proclamation is held together with the other four marks — it is not ranked above service, justice, or creation care but integrated with them. The Lausanne Movement's core documents have been foundational in articulating this holistic understanding of evangelism within global evangelicalism.

## Key Claims

- Evangelism is the proclamation of God's reign, not merely a recruitment strategy
- Proclamation and social action are inseparable dimensions of mission
- The Lausanne Covenant (1974) and its successors have shaped global evangelical mission thinking for 50 years
