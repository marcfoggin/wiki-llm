## Overview

The first mark of mission: to proclaim the good news of the Kingdom. Evangelism is the announcement that God's reign has broken into the world through Jesus Christ, bringing reconciliation, hope, and transformation. It is not reducible to a technique or programme but is the church's witness to what God has done and is doing.

Chris Wright defines evangelism as **'gospelling' the good news**—telling the whole biblical story of God's promise and accomplishment through Christ. It means proclaiming that God, having created the world, has acted to save it from sin and evil through Christ's death and resurrection; that the risen Jesus is now ascended Lord; and that he will return as Judge and King to establish his eternal reign of justice and peace in a renewed creation.

Within the Five Marks framework, proclamation is held together with the other four marks — it is not ranked above service, justice, or creation care but integrated with them. All five marks depend on a single foundation: **the Lordship of Christ over all creation**. In evangelism, we proclaim this lordship; in the other marks, we embody it. The Lausanne Movement's core documents have been foundational in articulating this holistic understanding of evangelism within global evangelicalism.

## Key Claims

- Evangelism is the proclamation of God's reign, not merely a recruitment strategy
- Proclamation and social action are inseparable dimensions of mission
- The Lausanne Covenant (1974) and its successors have shaped global evangelical mission thinking for 50 years
