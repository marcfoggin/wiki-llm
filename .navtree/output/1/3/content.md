## Overview

The third mark of mission: to respond to human need by loving service. This goes beyond charity to embodied compassion — meeting people in their actual circumstances with practical care. The theological foundation is incarnational: Christ entered human suffering, and the church is called to do the same.

Chris Wright locates compassion within Christ's example: we serve because "[Jesus] went about doing good" (Acts 10:38). Compassion is one of the two marks (with justice) grouped together as **serving society**. Wright argues that all five marks depend on Christ's Lordship; in compassion, we embody that lordship by following the example of the Lord Jesus. Service done in Christ's name participates in God's own mission to restore and redeem.

In pastoralist and mountain communities, loving service means understanding the specific needs shaped by mobility, remoteness, and marginalisation. Generic development programmes often miss these realities. Service grounded in Christ's Lordship attends carefully to context and treats people not as recipients of charity but as bearers of God's image deserving dignity and respect.

## Key Claims

- Loving service is incarnational — it embodies Christ's love in tangible, practical ways
- Serving others is serving Christ (Matthew 25:40)
- Authentic service creates credibility for witness — demonstrated sincerity rather than mere words
- Service must be shaped by the actual context and needs of the community, not by external assumptions
